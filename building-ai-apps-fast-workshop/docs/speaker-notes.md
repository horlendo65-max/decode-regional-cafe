# Speaker Notes

## Slide 1 — Welcome

Set expectations: this is a build-first workshop. Participants will use AI coding tools, but the final app still needs clear architecture and clean deployment.

## Slide 2 — The MVP promise

Explain that the target is not a perfect finance product. It is a deployable proof of concept that demonstrates value fast.

## Slide 3 — Why agents, skills and workflows

Use a simple analogy:

- Agent: team lead
- Skill: specialist
- Workflow: project plan

## Slide 4 — The use case

M-PESA statement analysis is familiar, practical and flexible. It also demonstrates how unstructured text becomes structured advice.

## Slide 5 — Architecture

Walk through frontend → API → workflow → agent → skills → Gemini.

## Slide 6 — Repo tour

Point out `starter/`, `reference/`, `.github/skills/` and `.github/prompts/`.

## Slide 7 — Lab flow

Participants should always move from setup to local run, then AI workflow, then deployment.

## Slide 8 — Prompt engineering

Emphasise role, task, context, rules and output format.

## Slide 9 — Deployment

Explain why environment variables must be configured separately on Vercel.

## Slide 10 — Demo

Keep demo short: paste sample statement, ask for categories, show savings advice.

## Slide 11 — Extensions

Show Health, Agriculture, FinTech and Daraja as modular extensions, not separate apps.

## Slide 12 — Lessons learned

Reinforce reusable architecture and responsible AI product thinking.

## Closing message

Participants now have a reusable AI MVP template they can adapt for multiple domains.
